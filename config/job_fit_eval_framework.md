# JUHI JAIN - JD EVALUATION FRAMEWORK
## For use in Job Search OS / Claude Code

---

## 9-POINT EVALUATION FORMAT (run for every JD)

1. Fit Score /10
2. Apply or Skip (with rationale)
3. Resume version (A = adtech, B = builder/AI)
4. Cover letter yes/no
5. Recruiter message (under 300 chars)
6. Product lead / hiring manager message (under 300 chars)
7. Referral angle (ISB alums, LG Ads alums, prior partners)
8. Career direction (right / neutral / reverse)
9. Location flag (if on-site outside Seattle)

---

## SKIP CRITERIA (hard rules)

- Score 5 or below: Skip, no cover letter
- Score 6: Apply, no cover letter
- Score 7+: Apply + think about cover letter
- 300+ applicants: Skip UNLESS score is 9+
- Comp top of range under $200K: Skip unless exceptional domain fit
- NYC, SF, LA on-site mandatory: Flag; Juhi will decide
- Denver, Mountain View on-site: Flag as location issue
- Hard domain requirements with no bridge (healthcare, physical security, fintech with no AI or adtech connection): Skip
- Coding/engineering hard requirements (Python, TypeScript production code): Skip
- Agency model (not product company): Skip
- Sales/revenue roles (not product): Skip
- Consumer marketplace with no B2B bridge: Skip

## SCORING RUBRIC

### Domain Match (0-3 points)
- 3: Direct match: CTV, programmatic, identity, adtech, AI platform, agentic AI, LLM tooling, applied AI, AI infra
- 2: Adjacent: retail media, martech, data platform, marketplace, developer tools, ML systems, identity adjacent
- 1: Transferable: B2B SaaS, growth, analytics, automation
- 0: No bridge: healthcare, physical security, fintech with no data/identity/AI angle

### AI Readiness of Role (0-3 points)
- 3: AI is core to the product: LLM, agents, agentic workflows, applied AI, AI infra, foundation models
- 2: AI is significant: ML-driven systems, AI features, data/automation heavy, decision support
- 1: AI is adjacent: "uses AI tools", automation mentioned but not core
- 0: No AI angle, pure traditional PM role

### Skills Match (0-2 points)
- 2: Core skills match: 0-1 building, identity, AI/ML collaboration, privacy/compliance, supply-demand, marketplace dynamics
- 1: Most match with 1-2 gaps
- 0: Hard requirements not met (production Python/TypeScript, LLM fine-tuning, RAG hands-on)

### Level & Scope (0-2 points)
- 2: Director/Principal/Staff/AD/GPM with cross-functional scope
- 1: Senior PM or Group PM
- 0: IC with no leadership or VP+ too senior

### TOTAL: 9 points
- 9-10: Tier 1, full effort
- 7-8: Tier 2, medium effort
- 5-6: Tier 3, spray
- Under 5: Skip

## COVER LETTER RULES

- Yes for 8+ fit scores AND under 200 applicants
- Yes for 7+ fit scores if role is highly strategic or referral available
- Format: Calibri 11pt, 0.75 inch margins
- No em dashes anywhere. Use hyphens (-) or rephrase.
- No pronoun "her"
- Use & not "and"
- Output as PDF + DOCX both
- Structure:
  1. Fit + honest gap acknowledgment if pivoting
  2. 3-4 evidence paragraphs with metrics tied to JD language
  3. Seattle location signal + genuine ask
- Never bullet points inside the letter - full paragraphs only
- Opening: never start with "I am excited to apply" - lead with insight or the match

## ACCURACY FLAGS (never overclaim)

- River OS scrapped post-LGE acquisition - SUCCESS story not failure
- $1M River OS revenue = pre-launch projection only, not realized
- App partner onboarding = contractual requirements, not commercial deal-closing
- Chatbot = Type-1 deterministic read-only diagnostic agent, NOT probabilistic RAG chatbot
- Cannot claim: production Python/TypeScript code, LLM fine-tuning, RAG pipeline hands-on, vector database implementation
- Job Hunt OS and Creative Approval Workflow are personal portfolio projects built with Claude Code, not shipped commercial products
- Can claim: agentic workflow design, LLM orchestration, prompt engineering, guardrails design, eval framework design
- Cannot claim: production deployment at scale, user base, revenue
- "Billions of impressions" for CTV scale - only use if can defend in interview

## JOB SEARCH HACK

When no referral available, message the hiring manager directly with a specific product opinion.
"I looked at your product and think you should do X."
Does not have to be right. Shows product thinking. Skips small talk. Jumps to round 2-3 of interview.

Netflix angle: "Your first-party signal is stronger than almost anyone in CTV but closing the identity match rate gap with programmatic demand is where the yield unlock is. I spent 18 months solving exactly this at LG Ads - 0 to 60% coverage, 3X bid rates, $3M incremental."

## LINKEDIN OUTREACH RULES

- Recruiter messages: under 300 characters always
- If accepted but no response after 7-10 days: one follow-up only
- Follow-up: "Hi [Name], just following up on my note from last week. Happy to share more context if helpful. Thanks!"
- One follow-up maximum then move on
- For hiring managers: lead with product opinion not job ask
- For corp dev / non-product contacts: build relationship first
